## Vision Statement

LifeOS is a personal operating system — not just a portfolio website.

It publicly demonstrates Rory's ability to build, document, test, and ship real software. It privately runs Rory's career, projects, daily tasks, finances, and AI workflow in a single place.

One codebase. One repository. One URL. A living system that grows alongside the person who uses it.

---

## Why This Exists

Most developer portfolios are static one-pagers that go stale. Most productivity tools are bloated, expensive, or generically designed for teams. LifeOS solves both problems at once.

The public portfolio shows employers and collaborators what Rory can build, how decisions are made, and what quality looks like from start to finish — including planning, requirements, QA, and deployment.

The private dashboard replaces the scattered collection of sticky notes, spreadsheets, and bookmarked job boards with a single purpose-built system. Every time the dashboard is used, the portfolio stays maintained. The project never dies because it has real daily utility.

## The Seven Areas — Purpose

### 1. Public Portfolio
Show who Rory is, what has been built, and why it matters to employers.
Sections: Hero, About, Skills, Projects, Case Studies, Resume/Experience, Contact.

### 2. Dashboard Home
A single-page "daily brief" that summarises every area at a glance — today's tasks, open job applications, budget balance, active projects, saved prompts, and a weekly focus note.

### 3. Task Tracker
A personal task manager for daily, project-level, and career tasks. Categories keep work, projects, and personal tasks separated. Priority and due dates drive what to focus on.

### 4. Prompt Library
A searchable personal database of reusable AI prompts — for coding, documentation, QA, job applications, and daily planning. Copy-to-clipboard keeps the workflow fast.

### 5. Job Application Tracker
Every application, every status, every follow-up — in one table. Prevents losing track across a busy job search. Surfaces which applications need attention today.

### 6. Budget Tracker
Simple income and expense tracking. Not a full accounting tool — just enough to see the monthly balance and understand where money is going, without opening a spreadsheet.

### 7. Projects / Case Studies
The portfolio proof layer. Each project has a problem statement, the solution built, the tech stack, screenshots, links, lessons learned, and what comes next. Feeds directly into the public portfolio's case study section.

## Core Values

**Useful before impressive.** The dashboard must solve real daily problems first. Three.js and AI features come after the task tracker works.

**Free by default.** No paid subscriptions, no paid APIs, no paid templates. Anyone with a GitHub account and no budget can reproduce this.

**Documentation as a visible skill.** The docs folder is not a formality — it is part of the portfolio. Requirements, architecture, QA plans, and decision logs are as important as the code.

**Build thin, then deep.** The thinnest version that works gets shipped first. Complexity is added only after the base is solid.

**Honest quality.** Tests either pass or fail. Deployment either works or it does not. No fabricated results. No "should work."

## What Success Looks Like in 6 Months

- The public portfolio is live on Vercel and is actively shared in job applications.
- The dashboard is used every day — tasks are tracked, applications are logged, prompts are saved.
- The GitHub repository shows consistent commit history, clean PRs, and maintained documentation.
- At least two case studies exist, including the LifeOS project itself.
- An employer who reviews the repository can understand every decision made and every quality standard applied — without asking questions.

## What Success Looks Like in 12 Months

- Supabase persistence is live. Data is saved across devices and sessions.
- Authentication protects the private dashboard.
- At least one AI-assisted feature is live (prompt improver, cover letter helper, or weekly summary).
- A visually distinctive 3D landing page sets the portfolio apart from generic templates.
- The project is referenced in interviews as a complete example of product thinking, documentation, and technical delivery.

---

## What This Is Not

- Not a SaaS product for other users.
- Not a platform with public sign-up.
- Not a blogging or social publishing tool.
- Not a showcase for frameworks or libraries for their own sake.
- Not a replacement for a full accounting system or full project management tool.
